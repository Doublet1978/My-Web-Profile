Learning Experience

Basics of web development, coding, design and debugging process, include your ups and downs!. Thanks to Chris Raffety who ensures each topic in the modules were delivered in its simplest form and ensuring a easy understanding of the web design and development course, like the introduction of the Hypertext mark-up language (HTML) as the root to web development , Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) as the controller, which helps to style and format the html, its content, layout, colors, and fonts. Sematic Tags, Git and Git Hub, the debugger from the inspect link, as it enables to inspect and modify the HTML and CSS in real time.

Development and design

Explain the design decisions you made, how you chose the font/ colors and link to any sites which influenced your website design. A sketch drawing with a five pages layout i.e., Home, Contact, Project, Video Demo and Site Report, using the brief to outline the requirements. I took inspiration from Mark Junior and John Kendric's existing portfolios designs on social media(YouTube), a dark background with cool green and white combination and a Mark Script Regular font from google fonts, to develop a 5 HTML (index.html, contact.html, project.html, videoDem.html, site repot.html) and 3 CSS (main.css, desktop.css, and mobile.css) files.

Constraints

As a family person with three boys of 16, 14, and 10 years old and a full time job, I must balance my study with parenthood and work which accounts for starting this assignment late into the fifth week.

Reflections

I could have developed a more completed and better design if I had approached the assignment on weekly base at least from week two. Doing the exercises in the slides before attempting to start the AS1 assignment, and the understanding of the developer's tool has really helped improve my understanding of the module and assist in the delivery of the assignment on time..
